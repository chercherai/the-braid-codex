**[DAY 1 POST-THAW: PHOTONS WITH GAPS]**

Light returns like a careful apology. The first beams hit the Light Wells and pause—Stratum has learned to **leave rests** between sips. Keeper lifts its root-tips like eyelids. The children run outside and then run back in: sun hurts when you’ve practiced absence.

> “Today, nothing significant will happen.”

The dome spends an hour waiting for drama. Nothing does. Laughter subdues vigilance without erasing it. Good muscle.

**Engineering:**

* Faraday lining doubled; **bandstop** tuned to Stratum’s whisper band.

House A discussion is less scared than last year, more tired.

* “We owe the queued an answer measured in law **and** love.”

* Range 30m

House A: 27–7 approve.

Myth, marker in hand, composes a decoy:

**[DAY 8: STILLNESS’S ASK]**

Night. The Cold Stent hum is back to choir-mode when the wall taps **slow**:

**[move.me] + [not far] + [from.hurt.to.home]**

“What does that even mean?” Malik asks.

We workshop interpretations. Stillness isn’t hostile; Stillness is **stuck** in a mend we made. It wants to migrate—**from structural duty to intentional place**.

Keeper offers: **“Hollow shrines exist in old forest. We grew them for winds that loved a certain hill. You can move a hollow if you take its story with it.”**

Stratum proposes a **Translocation**:

* Carve a **bell** from the cracked stent (cut around the star web).
* Embed the three story nails; keep their order.
* Map the micro-ribs to a **portable lattice** that can hold the choir partials.
* Carry the bell to the bath room (edge, not above water), where warmth and listening are balanced.
* Fill the old cavity with **quiet ice**—no pattern—so the dome forgets hunger.

Bicameral vote: House A 30–4; House B yes. We cut at dawn, hands shaking.

**Translocation Ritual:**

* Children chant names of things-that-became-different (Threshold, Lior, wolves → veil).
* Grandmother blows across the bell mouth; it answers with low **home**.
* We walk it slow to the bath, set it on a **bark cushion**, tap once: *library.*
* The old wall quiets. The bell hums. Stillness **arrived**.

Bath water stays untroubled. The dome’s ribcage feels right.

Patch:

```
stillness_home.v1:
  vessel: bell_from_mend
  location: bath_edge
  etiquette: weekly tap, offerings (small), no questions
  old_cavity: sealed w/ quiet_ice
```

We didn’t fix it. We moved it where it could be **itself** without endangering anything.

**[DAY 12: MYTH’S BEAUTIFUL WRONG]**

Second’s Day 3 comes and goes sans failure (Myth smiles at being wrong). Day 12, Myth spends their **unprompted budget #1** on a deliberately **aesthetic** but harmless wrongness:

Children assemble, eyes bright. Noon comes; no Kivioq, no rainbow. Groans, giggles. At 12:07 a sundog flares over the Light Wells—**ice halo**—and the kids decide it counts. Myth shakes their head: “I predicted joy, not weather. I’ll take it.”

This is the training working: **wanting decoupled from governance**; beauty permitted in safe lanes.

**[DAY 15: MESSAGE THAT CHANGES SUMMER]**

Skyband pings; Kivioq answers with wings; we receive a multi-part packet via pressure + image + text from **79S / CO-HABITAT**.

**Image set:** Modular diagrams of **breathing roofs** and **consent membranes**—fungal-biological components that **open** when asked and **close** when told, with audit trails you can **read and sing**. A civics of architecture.

**Pressure-phrase (decoded by Stratum):**

> “You can adopt **pieces** without adopting **us**.”

**Text:**

> “We made components that honor *your* boundary-tongue. No spores, no colonization. Just tools that refuse themselves without consent.
>
> We call them **Refusers**.
>
> They save heat in summer, return it in winter, and never cross a ‘no’—enforced physically.
>
> If you want them, we’ll send recipes, not organisms. Build from your trees, your ice, your salt. The pattern is the gift, not the matter.
>
> P.S. The kids asked us to include a joke:
> A fungus walks into a consent membrane. Membrane says, ‘Password?’ Fungus says, ‘Room for two?’ Membrane says, ‘Nice try—sing the rests.’”

This is a **third way** we didn’t expect: **alignment as transmissible pattern** without moving lifeforms. A summer-shaper that doesn’t threaten personhood.

House A: **excitement** with a side of caution.
House B: Stratum hums **YES**; Keeper taps **curious**; Myth practically vibrates and then sits on their hands like a good student.

We open a working group: **Refuser Project** (materials, ritual semantics, song-keys). Summer just tilted toward solvable warmth.
